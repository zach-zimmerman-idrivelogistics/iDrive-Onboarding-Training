Module 1

Industry Lingo

Before you can operate confidently at iDrive Logistics, you have to speak the language. This module covers the real terms, acronyms, and system names used every day across our team and with our 3PL partners.

🏢 Company & Entity Names

TermFull Name / Meaning
iDrive / IDLiDrive Logistics — what we go by. This is our brand and how we introduce ourselves to clients, carriers, and partners in every context.
IDFTMiDrive Fulfillment Transportation Management — our legal name as it appears on our W9 and formal financial/legal documents. Not used in day-to-day conversation.
IDFiDrive Fulfillment — a separate 3PL (warehouse that fulfills orders for clients). We own only a small portion of this company. We do not say we are iDrive Fulfillment — we are the logistics side only. Always identify yourself as iDrive Logistics.
⚠️ Identity Rule — Know This Cold

When speaking to anyone — clients, carriers, prospects, or partners — you are iDrive Logistics. Never introduce yourself as "iDrive Fulfillment." IDF is a related but distinct entity, and we are only a small stakeholder. Confusing the two creates legal, commercial, and reputational risk.

🖥️ Internal Systems & Platforms

TermFull Name / Meaning
KrakeniDrive's internal TMS platform. Always referred to as "TMS" externally — never "Kraken."
TMSTransportation Management System — manages shipping rates, carrier connections, label generation, and shipment history. Powered by Kraken.
SMC / IICShipment Management/Contract system — where carrier contracts, billing accounts, and markups are configured. The billing source of truth. 🔄 Being rebranded to IIC — iDrive Information Center
EasyPost / EZYPThird-party multi-carrier API platform used to connect carrier accounts to the TMS for rating and label generation.
NetSuiteAccounting/ERP system used for financials and vendor payments. Requires DUO multi-factor authentication.
HubSpotCRM system — used to track sales and closed-won customer data.
ExtensivA WMS platform used by some 3PL partners. Also referenced in the context of a TMS integration. Previously known as 3PL Central.
Zenventory / ZenA WMS platform used by some 3PL partners.
PackiyoA WMS platform used by some 3PL partners.
LogiwaA WMS platform used by some 3PL partners (e.g., 3PL Pros).
ShipStationA WMS/shipping platform used by some 3PL partners.
DeposcoA WMS platform used by some 3PL partners.
TrackstarA sync/integration tool that pulls inventory, products, and orders from a WMS (like Extensiv) into the TMS.
⚠️ External-Facing Rule

Always call the TMS "TMS" with clients — never "Kraken." Also note: SMC is being rebranded to IIC (iDrive Information Center) — you may hear both names during the transition period. Use IIC in all new documentation.

🏗️ Business Structure Terms

TermMeaning
3PLThird-Party Logistics provider — a warehouse/fulfillment company that ships on behalf of brands. iDrive's customers are often 3PLs.
3PL ParentThe top-level account representing a 3PL company (e.g., Gold3PL LLC, Warehouse Republic).
TenantA system-level account within the TMS/SMC (IIC) structure. 3PLs are set up as tenants; their brand clients are child tenants underneath them.
BrandAn individual e-commerce company that ships through a 3PL. Brands are the end clients of the 3PLs.
BYOC / BYOCABring Your Own Carrier / Account — when a 3PL or brand connects their own carrier account to the TMS instead of using iDrive's accounts.

🚚 Shipping & Freight Terms

TermMeaning
LTLLess Than Truckload — freight that doesn't fill an entire truck; multiple shippers share space.
TL / TruckloadFull Truckload — a freight shipment that fills an entire truck.
ParcelIndividual package shipments (UPS, FedEx, USPS, etc.) as opposed to freight/pallets.
BOLBill of Lading — official shipping document accompanying freight/LTL/truckload shipments. Signed by the driver at pickup.
eBOLElectronic Bill of Lading — a BOL submitted digitally through a carrier's portal.
PRO NumberA carrier-assigned tracking number for freight/LTL shipments.
VRIDAmazon-specific reference number required on BOLs for Amazon truckload pickups.
PalletA flat wooden platform used to stack and transport freight.
CrossdockInbound shipments transferred directly to outbound trucks with minimal warehousing.
ManifestClosing out the day's shipments with a carrier. Generates the pickup BOL or closeout record.
FCFSFirst Come, First Served — no set pickup appointment; drivers served in order of arrival.
INTLInternational shipment.
HazmatHazardous materials — packages with regulated substances. Require special carrier approvals.
UN#UN Number — 4-digit code for hazardous materials. Required on SKUs and labels for hazmat shipments.
ReeferRefrigerated truck/trailer for temperature-controlled freight.
DASDelivery Area Surcharge — additional fee for deliveries to rural or remote ZIP codes.
Residential SurchargeExtra fee when delivering to a home address vs. a commercial address.
ZoneShipping distance designation (1–8) used to calculate base freight rates.
Dims / Dimensional WeightCalculated weight based on package size (L × W × H ÷ divisor) used when greater than actual weight.
Rate ShopComparing carrier rates to find the best price. The TMS does this automatically.
MarkupPercentage added on top of the carrier's base rate that iDrive charges clients. Configured in SMC/IIC.
ContractA carrier account record in SMC/IIC linking a carrier account number to billing, rates, and markup settings.

📊 Billing & Reporting Terms

TermMeaning
PLDParcel Level Detail — a shipment-level data report used for billing reconciliation.
Unified PLDA combined PLD report across multiple accounts or 3PLs used for auditing charges.
Invoice ReconciliationComparing what a carrier billed iDrive against what the TMS shows was shipped, to catch discrepancies.
CRIDCustomer Registration ID — a USPS identifier tied to the billing/postage account.
MIDMailer ID — a USPS identifier assigned to each 3PL for USPS Direct API billing.

🏭 Warehouse & Operations Terms

TermMeaning
WMSWarehouse Management System — software a 3PL uses to manage inventory, orders, and shipping.
JITJust-In-Time — refers to specific fulfillment locations operated by iDrive.
JITMJIT Milpitas — iDrive's Just-In-Time fulfillment location in Milpitas, CA.
JITRJIT Reno — iDrive's Just-In-Time fulfillment location in Reno, NV.
SKUStock Keeping Unit — a unique identifier for a specific product in a warehouse.
Pickup WindowScheduled time range when a carrier arrives at a warehouse to collect packages or pallets.
PlacardA label placed on the outside of a pallet identifying the carrier, shipment info, or hazmat class.

Onboarding Flow

The step-by-step process for getting a new client from signed agreement to live and shipping on iDrive's platform.

🗺️ The iDrive Client Onboarding Journey

1
Deal Closed — CRM UpdateMark the deal "Closed Won" in HubSpot immediately. Update with contract value, service lines, and expected go-live date. Notify your team lead via Slack.
2
Agreement Sent via DocuSign / PandaDocSend the master service agreement and rate confirmation. Set a 24-hour follow-up task if not signed.
3
Slack Channel CreatedSet up a dedicated client Slack channel (#client-[companyname]). Add the client contact, ops lead, and billing rep.
4
Account Configuration in EasyPost, SMC/IIC & TMSOps provisions the client's shipping account — carrier credentials, rate tables, markup settings, and label configurations are set up across EasyPost, SMC/IIC, and the TMS.
5
Integration SetupIf the client has a WMS or e-commerce platform, the integration team begins the connector setup (Extensiv, ShipStation, Shopify, NetSuite, etc.).
