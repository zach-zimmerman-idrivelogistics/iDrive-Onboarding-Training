Module 1 — Locate the Alias Email List
Claims Email Setup

Locate the Alias Email List

Before creating a claims email, you need to access the shared alias email spreadsheet in Google Drive. This sheet tracks all assigned emails and is your reference before adding a new one.

💬
Step 1 — Open Slack & Find the Drive Link
  • 1
    Open Slack
    Launch Slack if it isn't already open. You'll be starting from the VAP Onboarding Team chat.
  • 2
    Open the VAP Onboarding Team Chat
    Navigate to the vap-onboarding-team channel in your Slack sidebar under Starred.
  • 3
    Click the Google Drive Links at the Top
    At the top of the VAP Onboarding Team chat, you'll see pinned or posted Google Drive links. Click those links to open the shared folder.
  • 4
    Open the Alias Email List
    Find and open the Alias Email List from the Google Drive folder. This takes you to the spreadsheet where all alias and claims emails are tracked.
✅ Good to Know

You should now see a Google Sheets page with access to the shared email tracking spreadsheet. If you receive a permissions error, contact Kaylynn Caldwell to have access granted before proceeding.

Claims Email Setup

Identify the Client's WMS

The claims email name includes the WMS type. Before creating the email, you need to verify which WMS the client uses by checking the #closed-won-customers Slack channel.

📋
Step 2 — Check the Alias Tab & Find the WMS
  • 1
    Navigate to the "Alias for Claims Inbox" Tab
    At the bottom of the Google Sheets spreadsheet, locate and click the tab labeled Alias for Claims Inbox. Make sure you are on this specific tab before proceeding.
  • 2
    Go to the #closed-won-customers Slack Channel
    Open Slack and navigate to the #closed-won-customers channel. Find the HubSpot post for the customer you are currently setting up a claims email for.
  • 3
    Note the WMS Listed for This Customer
    In the closed-won post, expand Show more / Show less if needed and locate the WMS field. Note the exact WMS name — e.g. Packiyo. You will use this in the email address.
⚠️ Important

The WMS name in the email address must match exactly what is listed in the #closed-won post. Using the wrong name or abbreviation will cause a mismatch in the tracking sheet.

Claims Email Setup

Find the Shipping State

The claims email address also includes the state abbreviation of the client's shipping facility. You'll locate this from the onboarding PDF inside Outlook.

✉️
Step 3 — Check Outlook for the Onboarding Email
  • 1
    Open Outlook
    Open Microsoft Outlook and navigate to the Onboarding email inbox in the left sidebar. This is the shared inbox where completed DocuSign agreements arrive.
  • 2
    Find the Closed Email for This Customer
    Search for or scroll to find the same customer's completed DocuSign email. Look for a subject line beginning with "Completed:" from Docusign.
  • 3
    Open the Info PDF Attachment
    Inside that email, locate the 3PL Onboarding Form PDF attachment (there may be two — open the one labeled v1). Click it to preview.
  • 4
    Note the State Under Shipping Information
    In the PDF preview, find the Shipping Information section at the top. Look at the City, State, Zip code row and note the state abbreviation (e.g. MI). You will use this in the email address.

📌 Write down both the WMS name (from Module 2) and the state abbreviation (from this step) before moving on. You'll need both to create the email in the next module.

Claims Email Setup

Create the Claims Email Address

Now that you have the WMS name and the state abbreviation, you're ready to create the claims email address and capture an annotated screenshot to send to Kaylynn.

📧
Step 4 — Build & Screenshot the Email Address

Use the following format to construct the claims email address:

📋 Email Format
WMS-companyname-state-claims@idrivelogistics.com
Example: Packiyo-Encore-MI-claims@idrivelogistics.com
  • 1
    Create the Email Address
    Using the WMS, company name, and state you've collected, construct and enter the claims email address in the appropriate field.
  • 2
    Take a Screenshot — Windows + Shift + S
    Press Windows + Shift + S. A snipping overlay appears. Drag to capture the relevant area of the screen. The screenshot is automatically copied to clipboard and a notification pops up.
  • 3
    Click the Snipping Tool Notification
    Click the Snipping Tool notification that appears in the bottom-right corner (or open it from the notification center by clicking the timestamp). This opens the screenshot in the editor.
  • 4
    Click the Highlighter Icon & Copy Icon
    In the Snipping Tool toolbar, click the highlighter icon (yellow marker). Use it to highlight the relevant fields. Then click the copy icon (top right) to copy the annotated screenshot to clipboard.

Message Kaylynn in Slack

With your annotated screenshot copied to clipboard, you'll now send Kaylynn Caldwell a Slack DM requesting she create the claims email — including the sheet link and screenshot.

💬
Step 5 — Send the Request to Kaylynn
  • 1
    Search for Kaylynn Caldwell in Slack
    In the Slack search bar at the top, type Kaylynn Caldwell and click her name to open a direct message.
  • 2
    Paste the Screenshot — Ctrl + V
    Click in the message text box and press Ctrl + V to paste the annotated screenshot you copied in the previous module.
  • 3
    Copy & Paste the Google Sheet Link
    Go back to the Google Sheet (Alias Email List), copy the URL from the browser address bar, and paste it into the Slack message alongside the screenshot.
  • 4
    Press Ctrl + Enter for a New Line
    Hold Ctrl + Enter to start a new line without sending. Type your message below the link.
  • 5
    Type Your Message & Send
    Type a short request — or use the example below — then verify the screenshot and link are both present and click Send.
💬 Suggested Message
Good afternoon Kaylynn! Can you create this claims email when you have time? Thanks!
📎 Before sending, verify: The Google Sheet link is in the message AND the annotated screenshot is attached. Both are required.

Claims Email Setup

Update the WMS Google Sheet

The final step is to log the new claims email in the WMS Onboarding tracking sheet, then proceed to Easypost setup for this specific email address.

📊
Step 6 — Log the Email in the Tracking Sheet
  • 1
    Return to the Google Sheet
    Go back to the Google Sheets spreadsheet you were working in (the Alias Email List).
  • 2
    Switch to the WMSOnboarding Tab
    At the bottom of the spreadsheet, click the WMSOnboarding tab. Make sure you are on this tab — not the Alias for Claims Inbox tab from earlier.
  • 3
    Add the Business Name to the Next Open Row
    Find the next empty row and add the business name for the company you're onboarding. This reserves their slot and keeps the sheet in order.
📌 Note for Training

Because the example company uses Packiyo, the training exercise uses the next available email slot. For your actual onboarding, use whatever company you are currently setting up — do not use the training example company in the live sheet.

⚡
Next Step — Proceed to Easypost Setup

Now that the claims email has been designated and logged, follow the Easypost Training to get this specific email set up in Easypost.

📬 Because the claims email is formatted as WMSOnboarding@idrivelogistics.com, the Easypost verification code will be sent to the WMSOnboarding email in your Outlook. Ensure Kaylynn has given you access to the relevant onboarding emails before starting. Follow the Easypost instructions exactly.

✅ You're All Set

Once Easypost setup is complete for this claims email, the claims email onboarding for this client is done. Good luck!
